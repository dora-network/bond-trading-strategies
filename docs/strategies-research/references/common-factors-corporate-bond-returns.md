# Common Factors in Corporate Bond Returns

**URL:** https://www.aqr.com/-/media/AQR/Documents/Journal-Articles/Common-Factors-in-Corporate-Bond-Returns.pdf

**Authors:** Israel, Palhares, Richardson (AQR Capital Management)

## What It Covers

Empirical analysis of factor investing in US corporate bonds (1997-2015), constructing factor portfolios from over 10,000 bonds. Tests whether equity-style factors (value, momentum, carry, defensive) translate to credit markets.

## Key Claims

1. **Carry, defensive, momentum, and value** all have positive risk-adjusted expected returns in corporate bonds *(extracted)*
2. **Combined multi-factor portfolio** achieves Sharpe ratio of 2.19 (long-short) with low correlations among factors *(extracted)*
3. **Bond momentum is partially independent** of equity momentum on the same issuer (~0.35 correlation) — ~2/3 of the signal is credit-specific *(extracted)*
4. **Carry** as a stand-alone variable does not exhibit reliable association with future excess returns but is marginally significant when controlling for other characteristics *(extracted)*
5. **Long-only factor-tilted portfolios** yield net active return of 2.20% annualized (IR 0.86) after transaction costs *(extracted)*
6. **Momentum** shows the strongest evidence of mispricing as its source, while carry appears risk-based *(inferred)*
7. **Value in bonds** uses spread relative to default probability estimates as the anchor — fundamentally different from equity book-to-market *(extracted)*

## Limitations

- Sample ends in 2015; post-GFC era of QE may alter factor behavior
- Transaction cost estimates may be optimistic for less liquid bonds
- Does not address capacity constraints of systematic credit strategies
