# Carry Trade and Momentum in Currency Markets

**URL:** https://www.kellogg.northwestern.edu/faculty/rebelo/htm/carry.pdf

**Authors:** Rebelo et al. (Kellogg/Northwestern)

## What It Covers

Empirical properties of carry trade and momentum strategies in currency markets. Reviews three explanations: risk compensation, rare disasters/peso problems, and price pressure.

## Key Claims

1. **Carry trade:** Borrow low-interest-rate currencies, lend high-interest-rate currencies. Average excess return ~4.2% with Sharpe 0.65 *(extracted)*
2. **Momentum strategy:** Go long currencies with positive recent returns, short those with negative returns. Average excess return ~4.5% with Sharpe 0.62 *(extracted)*
3. **50-50 combined strategy:** Sharpe ratio 0.98 — driven by low correlation between carry and momentum payoffs *(extracted)*
4. **Diversification across currencies substantially improves** risk-adjusted returns for both strategies *(extracted)*
5. **Carry trade is motivated by failure of Uncovered Interest Parity (UIP)** — high-interest-rate currencies do not depreciate as UIP predicts *(extracted)*
6. **Momentum made money during the 2008 financial crisis** — this argues against rare-disaster explanations for its profitability *(extracted)*
7. **Price pressure** (wedge between marginal and average payoffs) is a promising but unproven explanation for strategy profitability *(extracted)*

## Direct Translation to Continuous Bond Markets

In a continuous fractional bond market:
- **Bond carry trade:** Borrow at short-term rate, invest in higher-yielding bonds (direct analog to FX carry)
- **Bond momentum:** Long bonds with positive recent excess returns, short underperformers
- **Combined bond carry+momentum** would likely show similarly low correlation as in FX
- **UIP failure in FX → Expectations Hypothesis failure in bonds** — the same economic mechanism
- **Cross-sectional diversification** across many bonds (issuers, maturities) would improve Sharpe as diversification does in FX across currencies

## Limitations

- FX-specific; direct translation to bonds requires adaptation for duration, credit risk
- Sample period ends before 2020
