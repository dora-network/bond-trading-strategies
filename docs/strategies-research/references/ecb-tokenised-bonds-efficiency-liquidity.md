# ECB: Tokenised Bonds — Efficiency and Liquidity

**URL:** https://www.ecb.europa.eu/press/financial-stability-publications/macroprudential-bulletin/html/ecb.mpbu202604_03.en.html

**Published:** April 2026

## What It Covers

ECB empirical analysis of whether tokenisation improves bond issuance efficiency and market liquidity. Matched-pair comparison of tokenised vs conventional bonds.

## Key Claims

1. **Yield spread at issuance is 0.14pp lower** for tokenised bonds — 40% average reduction vs comparable conventional bonds, significant at 5% *(extracted)*
2. **Bid-ask spread is 0.05pp lower** for tokenised bonds — 27% average reduction, significant at 5% *(extracted)*
3. **Underwriting fees are 0.04pp higher** on average but not statistically significant *(extracted)*
4. **88% of issuances occurred in the last 3 years** — market is nascent but growing rapidly *(extracted)*
5. **24/7 trading and fractionalised ownership** could enable broader investor participation *(extracted)*
6. **Benefits depend on wider stakeholder participation** — institutional investors may see limited benefit from smaller transaction sizes *(extracted)*
7. **Transition costs may outweigh benefits short-term** — legacy and new infrastructure must coexist *(extracted)*

## Limitations

- Small sample size due to nascent market
- ECB/SNB trials may bias results (excluding trial bonds, 2025 issuance slightly lower than 2024)
- Long-term scalability unproven
