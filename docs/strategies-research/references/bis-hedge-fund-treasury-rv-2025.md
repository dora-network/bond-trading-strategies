# BIS: Hedge Fund Relative Value Trades in US Treasuries

**URL:** https://www.bis.org/publ/qtrpdf/r_qt2512y.htm

**Published:** December 2025 (BIS Quarterly Review)

## What It Covers

Analysis of hedge fund relative value trading strategies in US Treasury securities, focusing on the cash-futures basis trade and the growing interest rate swap (IRS) spread trade.

## Key Claims

1. **IRS swap spread trade** has grown from $281B (Q1 2024) to $631B (Q2 2025), more than doubling *(extracted)*
2. **Swap trade is now the primary driver** of hedge fund Treasury exposure growth, surpassing the cash-futures basis trade *(extracted)*
3. **Cash-futures basis trade** remains the largest strategy but has stagnated since early 2024 *(extracted)*
4. **April 2025 turbulence** caused 11% contraction in swap trade ($707B to $631B), revealing rate-shock sensitivity *(extracted)*
5. **IRS spread** (10-year swap rate minus 10-year UST yield) co-moves with inferred trade size — hedge funds enter when spread is wide *(extracted)*
6. **Regulatory expectations** (SLR relief for banks) drive significant positioning in the swap trade *(inferred)*
7. **International dimension** growing — similar incentives emerging in euro area and Japan *(extracted)*

## Limitations

- Size estimate is an upper bound; actual trade size may be smaller
- Only covers hedge fund activity visible in OFR data
- Does not model tail risk scenarios for simultaneous unwind
