# Kambhu: Convergence Trading in IRS Spreads

**URL:** https://www.newyorkfed.org/medialibrary/media/research/epr/06v12n1/0605kambpdf.pdf

**Author:** John Kambhu (Federal Reserve Bank of New York)

## What It Covers

Empirical analysis of convergence trading in the interest rate swap spread market. Tests whether trading losses affect convergence speed and whether repo volume shocks destabilize spreads.

## Key Claims

1. **Swap spread converges more slowly** when traders' capital (endowments) has been impaired by trading losses *(extracted)*
2. **Convergence trading both stabilizes AND destabilizes** — typically absorbs shocks, but unusually large shocks can be amplified by premature unwinding *(extracted)*
3. **Repo volume falls when convergence traders experience losses** — consistent with position unwinding *(extracted)*
4. **Swap spread tends to diverge from fundamental value when repo volume falls** and repo volume falls when traders lose money *(extracted)*
5. **Convergence coefficient is smaller** when inferred trading activity is lower (after unprofitable periods) *(extracted)*
6. **Trading risk**, as reflected in repo volume fluctuations, can on occasion destabilize the swap spread *(extracted)*

## Implications for Continuous Bond Markets

- **More participants = more potential for simultaneous unwinding** during stress
- **Continuous margining/repo** in a tokenized market could accelerate forced selling cascades
- **The stabilizing function of convergence traders persists** but is fragile — continuous markets may increase the frequency of destabilizing episodes
- **Lower transaction costs may attract more levered positions**, increasing systemic fragility

## Limitations

- Study period pre-dates electronic Treasury trading dominance
- Only covers swap spread, not other convergence trades
- Does not model HFT or algorithmic trading effects
