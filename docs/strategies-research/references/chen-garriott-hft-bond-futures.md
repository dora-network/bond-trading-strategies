# Chen & Garriott: HFT in Bond Futures

**URL:** https://www.banqueducanada.ca/wp-content/uploads/2018/02/swp2018-8.pdf

**Authors:** Marie Chen, Corey Garriott (Bank of Canada)

## What It Covers

Empirical study of HFT activity in Canadian Government bond futures using order-book data from the Montréal Exchange (2009-2015). Tests whether HFTs engage in back-running (predatory trading) against institutional trade strings.

## Key Claims

1. **HFTs do NOT back-run institutions** — HFT cumulative order flow is always opposite to the trade string direction (HFTs are net sellers to buy strings, buyers from sell strings) *(extracted)*
2. **HFTs supply liquidity** in small sizes (~$100K max counterparty position even against $100M+ trade strings) *(extracted)*
3. **Increased HFT competition (quasi-experiment Nov 2011) benefits institutions** — improved implementation shortfall, effective spreads, and short-term price impact *(extracted)*
4. **No back running observed even for "more-informed" positions** — $100M+ strings worked over >1 hour, or high-permanent-impact strings *(extracted)*
5. **HFTs improve per-trade execution cost at short term** (effective spreads) but not at longer term (5-min price impact) *(extracted)*
6. **HFTs hold counterparty position to <1% of trade string notional** — they provide liquidity but do not bear material quantity *(extracted)*
7. **HFT identification criteria:** high switching rate, zero overnight inventory 4 of 5 days, average order lifetime <1 minute *(extracted)*

## Implications for Continuous Bond Markets

- **HFT markets can function well for bonds** — the BrokerTec-style CLOB model translates
- **Institutional execution still works** — HFTs provide small but meaningful liquidity
- **No evidence of the predatory behavior feared in equity HFT** — at least in this market
- **Continuous trading + HFT = tighter spreads but** the depth of institutional liquidity (ability to execute large blocks) remains limited

## Limitations

- Single market (Canadian bond futures), single exchange
- Data ends 2015 — predates major electronification waves
- Only identifies back-running; other predatory strategies not tested
