## Key Takeaways

- Technical indicators ARE proven on bonds — 27,000 rule variants tested with systematic profitability, especially in EM
- Multi-indicator fusion (MACD + Bollinger + RSI) beats single indicators — confirmed on Treasury bonds
- Regime-adaptive parameters (CPO) are essential — fixed parameters fail when markets change
- Volume indicators (OBV, MFI, volume profile) become viable ONLY in continuous CLOB markets — a major new capability
- The yield-vs-price direction is THE critical bond-specific adaptation for all technical indicators
- Duration scaling is mandatory — TLT (17yr) has ~8× the price volatility of SHY (2yr)
- Institutional enhancement stack: volatility normalization → z-score transformation → regime classification → CPO → signal fusion
- The Kondor paradox still applies: more efficient markets may reduce profitability of simple technical strategies

## Flagged Contradictions

- More efficient markets (goal of continuous/fractional) may reduce technical indicator profitability
- Simple indicators work best in LESS efficient markets (EM sovereigns) but continuous markets aim for MORE efficiency
- Volume indicators enabled by continuous markets may compensate for reduced price-based predictability — the net effect is unknown
