# BIS: Tokenisation of Government Bonds

**URL:** https://www.bis.org/publ/bisbull107.pdf

**Authors:** Aldasoro, Cornelli, Frost, Koo Wilkens, Lewrick, Shreeti

## What It Covers

Empirical analysis of 39 tokenised bonds (24 corporate, 15 government/supranational) comparing them to matched conventional bonds. Examines liquidity, issuance costs, and design choices for tokenised bond markets.

## Key Claims

1. **Tokenised bonds have lower bid-ask spreads** — mean of 19 bps vs 30 bps for conventional bonds from same issuers *(extracted)*
2. **Issuance costs are comparable** — no systematic difference between tokenised and conventional *(extracted)*
3. **Minimum investment is significantly lower** — $110,000 avg for tokenised vs $185,000 for conventional *(extracted)*
4. **Two issuance types:** Native (single record on-platform) and conventional-issuance-with-digital-representation (backed by conventional bond held by custodian) *(extracted)*
5. **Market remains in infancy** — $8 billion total issuance to date *(extracted)*
6. **24/7 trading and near-instant settlement** are key potential benefits via smart contracts *(extracted)*
7. **Fractional ownership and programmable platforms** could broaden investor base and enable new collateral arrangements *(extracted)*

## Limitations

- Very early stage market; results may not scale
- Most issuance on permissioned DLT; public blockchain adoption limited
- Regulatory and infrastructure challenges remain substantial
